Privacy Policy for Keepsake

Last updated: June 2026

Keepsake is a recipe-keeping app. This policy explains what data the app handles, in plain terms.

Short version

Keepsake does not collect, store, or transmit any of your data anywhere. Everything you add to the app — recipe names, photos, stories, ingredients, instructions — stays only on your own device.

What the app does locally

When you add a recipe, Keepsake saves the text you enter and any photo you take directly to your phone's private storage. This information is never sent to a server, because Keepsake doesn't have one. There is no account, no sign-in, and no cloud sync.

Camera

Keepsake asks to use your camera only when you choose to photograph a recipe card. The photo is saved straight to the app's private folder on your device. Keepsake does not access your camera at any other time.

Third parties

Keepsake does not use analytics, advertising, or tracking services of any kind. No data is shared with anyone, because no data leaves your device in the first place.

Children's privacy

Keepsake does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children, since the app does not collect information at all.

Deleting your data

Uninstalling the app removes everything Keepsake stored on your device. You can also delete individual recipes at any time from within the app.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this same address with a new "last updated" date.
